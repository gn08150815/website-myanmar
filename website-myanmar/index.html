MYANMAR NEEDS YOU

War, Genocide, and Ethnic Cleansing–Millions of Innocent Civilians Displaced

In 2021, Myanmar’s military junta launched a successful coup against the newly elected government, the NLD (National League for Democracy) in order to take power in Myanmar. A civilian protest took place in the country’s capital, Naypyidaw, with protesters demanding the reestablishment of stolen democracy. Their cries were met with violence, as the military proceeded to kill 600 protesters. In retaliation, the pro-democracy citizens began to mobilize for war, forming the National Unity Government with pre-coup leaders and their military, the People’s Defense Force.

Despite this development, in the center of the conflict is 3.6 million displaced victims and families, forced to flee from their homes when conflict became too dangerous to stay. Including in that figure is an ethnic minority group, the Rohingya, who are undergoing ethnic genocide. Violence strengthened by a 1982 citizenship law that excluded citizen rights from the group, the Rohingya the largest stateless ethnic minority in the world. A recent 2017 military crackdown killing 6700 victims worsened their situation, and the 2021 installation of the very same military that sought their execution ensured risks of total genocide. Millions have already fled to countries like Bangladesh and Malaysia.

As it stands, there appears to be no end in sight, and as endless fighting continues, so will the relentless death toll.
